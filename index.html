June 8, 2025

If you want to get straight into sewing, I understand that. My coding skills aren't great yet, so just look for when the text turns pink. I woke up at 5:30 this morning to the sound of my petulent phone's alarm. I spent the night out with a friend from high school, so four hours of sleep and a mild hangover were not circumstances to wake up to. What's worse is that I'd slept with my phone in my bed, so finding the 2020 iPhone SE was a chore in and of itself. It had fallen off my bed onto the floor between my Queen-sized and the wall. By the time I was able to shut off the loud, almost mocking, ringtone, sleepiness had left my body. I know this doesn't have much to do with coding or sewing, but I think the contexts in which I decide to sew and code are maybe just as important as the coding and sewing itself. Everything about my environment and mood influences my so-called productivity, so just writing about how a day might have started off rough and still finding time to dedicate to the things that I am interested in learning maybe shows that any circumstance is a good circumstance to grow. Of course there exists extenuating circumstances where maybe breaks are needed, but those are not mine for now. At least, I don't believe so. ANYWAY, today I am planning on redoing my first ever sewing project. It's a tote bag. The first tote bag I'd made came out a little wonky, I didn't backstitch or finish any of the seams, but I felt immense pride about being able to sew something all by myself. Or, at least, I found a good video series to follow along with haha. If I'm honest, at first I did think that Sydney did talk quite a lot, but she has a really lovely personality and it's obvious that she's passionate about sewing. I think it might be more accurate to say that my attention span was not used to trying to really suck in new information from people from the internet. I am a product of the 2000s and one of the first iPad babies.

If you're like me and don't really want to do all that much reading or watch YouTube video after YouTube video to get started, I feel you. Capitalism and social media kills brain cells and patience.

Here are the tools I needed to get started:
  • cutting mat (24"x36")
  • sewing machine duh
  • thread
  • rotary cutter and/or scissors
  • 1 yard fabric for the exterior
  • 1 yard fabric for the lining
  • iron
  • straight edge
  • hot ruler
  • pins AND/OR clips
  • tailor's chalk OR hera marker
